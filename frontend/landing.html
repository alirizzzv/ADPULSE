01 · THE PROBLEM

What is Real-Time Bidding?

Every webpage load triggers a millisecond auction. Here's the challenge ADPULSE solves.

01

The Constraint

A fixed budget, and a decision in under 5ms per request: bid or not, and how much. Too aggressive and the budget dies early; too conservative and you lose every auction.

02

The Auction

A second-price auction — the highest bidder wins but pays the second-highest price. This rewards honest bidding: your best move is to bid your true value.

03

The Solution

Two LightGBM models predict click & conversion probability per request. A greedy strategy then prices each bid under the budget constraint.

03 · THE ENGINE

The Bidding Formula

How a raw bid price is computed for every single request.

bid=base_bid×CTR×(1 + N × CVR)

CTR acts as the primary gate — if click probability is low, the whole bid shrinks proportionally. The (1 + N × CVR) term scales the bid up for advertisers where conversions matter more. For advertiser 3476 (N=10), a likely converter can multiply the bid by up to 11×.

What is CTR?

Click-Through Rate — the probability a user clicks after seeing the ad. Typically ~0.1–0.2% in display advertising.

What is CVR?

Conversion Rate — given a click, the probability the user completes a desired action like a purchase.

What is N?

An advertiser-specific weight for how much a conversion is worth relative to a click. The score we maximise is Clicks + N × Conversions.
